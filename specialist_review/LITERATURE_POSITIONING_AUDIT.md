# Round 52 literature-positioning audit

Audit date: 2026-07-15

## Outcome

The literature update strengthens the manuscript by replacing older arXiv-only citations with formal publication metadata wherever a journal or book version is available. The closest conceptual clusters remain distinct but overlapping:

1. **Rational Gauss-type and pole-aware quadrature.** Gautschi’s chapter and JCAM survey establish that rational structure and external poles have long been incorporated into quadrature. Tomanović provides a more recent Gauss-type construction involving external denominator zeros. The present paper therefore cannot claim pole awareness or rational exactness as new.
2. **Generalized Gaussian and positive quadrature.** Huybrechs supplies a modern computational treatment for Chebyshev sets; positive interpolatory and optimization-based constructions further show that positivity and nonlinear moment matching are established themes.
3. **Rational minimax and interpolation constraints.** Filip et al. establish modern rational minimax computation, and Zhang–Zhang explicitly address interpolation-constrained rational minimax. “Minimax with constraints” is therefore not an adequate novelty claim.
4. **Markov/Stieltjes rational approximation and quadrature from rational approximants.** Beckermann et al. address relative rational approximation of Markov functions. Horning–Trefethen, now formally published in 2026, is the closest conceptual bridge from rational approximation to quadrature and the Gauss/Padé connection.
5. **Worst-case quadrature and positive kernel rules.** Trefethen, Goda–Kazashi–Tanaka, and Hayakawa show that worst-case error analysis and positive-weight model classes are established independently.
6. **Validated numerics.** Moore–Kearfott–Cloud, Rump, and Johansson now provide explicit methodological support for the interval Newton/Krawczyk and Arb claims.

## Defensible contribution after the update

The contribution that remains plausibly distinguishable is the **combined finite framework**:

- positive symmetric Stieltjes realization;
- continuum Lorentzian relative minimax;
- variable polynomial-moment depth at fixed node-pair count;
- exact mixed structural/smooth selector;
- computer-assisted exact-root and rounded-operator parameter-map certification.

This is a positioning statement, not a proof of global novelty. No search can rule out equivalent formulations under moment-space, generalized Gaussian, constrained Markov, multipoint Padé, or interpolatory rational terminology. External specialist prior-art review remains necessary.
