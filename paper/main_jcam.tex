\begin{abstract}
We consider fixed-budget quadrature for integrands containing a
nearly singular centered Lorentzian component and an incompletely
specified smooth residual. For \(n\) positive symmetric node pairs, a
moment-depth parameter \(k\), \(0\leq k\leq n\), imposes the first
\(k\) moment conditions at infinity. The endpoints are an unrestricted
positive Stieltjes rational minimax rule and a degree-\((2n-1)\)-exact
quadrature rule. Shared Laurent coefficients reduce the
difference-numerator degree to at most \(2n-k-1\), yielding a
\(2n-k+1\)-point alternation certificate within the stated admissible
class. Complete high-precision hierarchies for \(n=2,3,4\) quantify the
finite-budget price of polynomial protection. For smooth residuals with
Chebyshev coefficients bounded by \(C
ho^{-j}\), the exact worst-case
smooth error is the weighted defect norm
\(C\sum_j
ho^{-j}|I[T_j]-Q[T_j]|\). Adding the Lorentzian uncertainty
gives an exact model-aware rule-selection risk. Interval arithmetic
applied directly to the published rounded rules certifies a unique rule
on 99.9703\% of the tested parameter-domain area. A
shifted-Lorentzian stress test and a separate verification code path
delimit the scope and support reproducibility.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Polynomial exactness is an effective design principle for smooth
integrands, but it need not be the most efficient use of a fixed
evaluation budget when the dominant difficulty is a nearby pole.
Rationally exact quadrature incorporates prescribed singular structure
\cite{gautschi1993}, while generalized Gaussian constructions extend
exactness to non-polynomial function spaces \cite{huybrechs2017}.
Optimization-based and positive cubature methods provide further ways
to design nodes and weights \cite{keshavarzzadeh2018,glaubitz2020}.
Related rational approximation theory for Markov functions explains the
importance of negative real poles and positive residues
\cite{beckermann2021}.

The practically important intermediate case is partial trust: a
structured nearly singular component is expected, but an unknown smooth
background must also be protected. Exactness alone does not always
predict practical quadrature performance \cite{trefethen2021}. We
therefore introduce a hierarchy indexed by a moment depth \(k\). Small
\(k\) allocates more freedom to the structural family; large \(k\)
provides stronger low-degree background protection.

The contribution is not a claim that constrained rational approximation
or specialized quadrature is new in isolation. Interpolation-constrained
rational minimax methods and common-support cubature provide close prior
context \cite{zhang2025,bravo2023}. The proposed contribution is the
combined framework of:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
\item a positive partial moment-at-infinity hierarchy;
\item a class-specific alternation count;
\item an exact coefficient-space risk and rule selector;
\item interval certification of the distributed rounded rules.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Model and admissible hierarchy}

Let
\[
I[f]=\int_{-1}^{1}f(x)\,dx,\qquad
L_b(x)=\frac{1}{x^2+b^2},\qquad b\in[0.08,0.12].
\]
With \(s=b^2\) and \(y=x^2\),
\[
F(s)=I[L_{\sqrt{s}}]
=\int_0^1\frac{y^{-1/2}}{s+y}\,dy
=\frac{2}{\sqrt{s}}\arctan\frac{1}{\sqrt{s}}.
\]
A symmetric \(n\)-pair rule is
\[
Q[f]=\sum_{j=1}^{n}w_j\{f(-x_j)+f(x_j)\},
\quad 0<x_1<\cdots<x_n<1,\quad w_j>0.
\]
Writing \(y_j=x_j^2\), its response on the structural family is
\[
r_Q(s)=Q[L_{\sqrt{s}}]
=2\sum_{j=1}^{n}\frac{w_j}{s+y_j}.
\]

For \(0\leq k\leq n\), define \(\mathcal A_{n,k}\) by
\[
\sum_{j=1}^{n}w_jy_j^\ell=\frac{1}{2\ell+1},
\qquad \ell=0,\ldots,k-1.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Moment and polynomial exactness]
A symmetric rule belongs to \(\mathcal A_{n,k}\) if and only if it is
exact for every polynomial of degree at most \(2k-1\).
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Odd polynomials are integrated exactly by symmetry. For an even
monomial,
\[
Q[x^{2\ell}]=2\sum_{j=1}^{n}w_jy_j^\ell,
\qquad
I[x^{2\ell}]=\frac{2}{2\ell+1}.
\]
The stated equivalence follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Laurent matching]
A rule belongs to \(\mathcal A_{n,k}\) if and only if
\[
r_Q(s)-F(s)=O(s^{-k-1}),\qquad s\to\infty.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Use the finite geometric expansion
\[
\frac{1}{s+y}
=\sum_{\ell=0}^{m-1}(-1)^\ell y^\ell s^{-\ell-1}
+O(s^{-m-1}),
\]
whose remainder is uniform for \(y\in[0,1]\). Integrating against
\(y^{-1/2}\,dy\) and summing against the discrete measure
\(2\sum_jw_j\delta_{y_j}\) gives the two Laurent expansions.
\end{proof}

\section{Degree reduction and alternation}

Write \(r_i=p_i/q_i\), where \(q_i\) is monic of degree \(n\).

\begin{proposition}[Difference-numerator degree]
If \(r_1,r_2\in\mathcal A_{n,k}\), then
\[
r_1-r_2=\frac{H}{q_1q_2},\qquad
\deg H\leq 2n-k-1,
\]
unless \(H\equiv0\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The generic numerator \(p_1q_2-p_2q_1\) has degree at most \(2n-1\).
Shared moment conditions give \(r_1-r_2=O(s^{-k-1})\). Since \(q_1q_2\)
is monic of degree \(2n\), a nonzero numerator of degree \(d\) must
satisfy \(d-2n\leq-k-1\).
\end{proof}

Define the relative structural error
\[
\varepsilon_Q(s)=\frac{r_Q(s)}{F(s)}-1.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Alternation certificate]
Suppose \(Q^\star\in\mathcal A_{n,k}\) has \(2n-k+1\) ordered points
at which \(\varepsilon_{Q^\star}\) has equal magnitude and alternating
sign, and this magnitude equals its uniform norm. Then no
\(Q\in\mathcal A_{n,k}\) has strictly smaller uniform relative error.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A strictly better competitor would make
\(\varepsilon_{Q^\star}-\varepsilon_Q\) alternate sign at all
\(2n-k+1\) points, forcing at least \(2n-k\) distinct zeros. Because
\(F(s)>0\), these are zeros of \(r_{Q^\star}-r_Q\), contradicting the
degree bound unless the rational responses are identical.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The theorem establishes minimaxity within the stated class. It does not
by itself establish unconditional uniqueness or optimality over
adaptive algorithms or different node budgets.
\end{remark}

\section{Exact coefficient-space risk}

Let
\[
h(x)=\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}a_jT_j(x),
\qquad
|a_j|\leq C\rho^{-j},\quad \rho>1,
\]
and define
\[
\Delta_j(Q)=I[T_j]-Q[T_j].
\]

\begin{theorem}[Exact coefficient-box norm]
For every fixed bounded quadrature rule \(Q\),
\[
\sup_h|I[h]-Q[h]|
=C G_Q(\rho),
\qquad
G_Q(\rho)=\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}\rho^{-j}|\Delta_j(Q)|.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Uniform convergence permits termwise application:
\[
I[h]-Q[h]=\sum_{j=0}^{\infty}a_j\Delta_j(Q).
\]
The triangle inequality gives the upper bound. It is attained by
\(a_j=C\rho^{-j}\operatorname{sign}\Delta_j(Q)\).
\end{proof}

Define
\[
S_Q=\max_{b\in[0.08,0.12]}|I[L_b]-Q[L_b]|.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Exact mixed-class risk]
For \(f=h+cL_b\), with \(h\) in the coefficient box and
\(|c|\leq c_{\max}\),
\[
\sup_f|I[f]-Q[f]|=C G_Q(\rho)+c_{\max}S_Q.
\]
\end{theorem}

The signs of the smooth and structural extremizers can be chosen
independently. With \(\tau=c_{\max}/C\), the robust choice minimizes
\(G_Q(\rho)+\tau S_Q\).

\section{Certification of the rounded rules}

The ideal minimax rules satisfy the moments exactly. The distributed
decimal rules have tiny but nonzero rounding defects. The
coefficient-space risk remains exact for every fixed rounded operator,
but the ideal degree-reduction theorem is not inherited from a nominal
\(k\)-label alone.

We therefore certified the rounded rules directly. Structural maxima
were enclosed over the complete \(b\)-interval, Chebyshev defects were
enclosed through degree 500, the remaining tail was bounded
analytically, and a parameter cell was labeled only when one risk upper
bound was below every competing lower bound throughout the cell.

\begin{figure}[ht]
\centering

The unresolved area is 0.0297\%, localized in thin cells
intersecting transition curves rather than broad unvalidated regions.

\section{Independent verification and reproducibility}

A separate verifier reconstructs moments, alternation points, centered
errors, structural errors, transfer functions, and deterministic
selector checks from only the published nodes, weights, and formulas.
It does not import the nonlinear optimization or interval-certification
code.

All geometry and alternation checks passed. The largest constrained
moment residual of the rounded rules was
\(\num{5.551e-17}\). The maximum relative discrepancy in the
transfer-function audit was \(\num{2.731e-12}\), and all scored
certified selector spots agreed.

A one-command repository regenerates the principal tables and figures
and runs seven automated tests. This is an internal independent
reimplementation, not external third-party replication.

\section{Relation to prior work and contribution scope}

Simultaneous and shared-support quadrature constructions provide
additional context \cite{vanassche2023,bravo2023}. Recent lower-bound
work also emphasizes that upper-error estimates should be interpreted
relative to the function class \cite{goda2024}. Our claim is limited to
the combination of the partial moment-at-infinity hierarchy, its
alternation structure, the exact coefficient-space selector, and direct
certification of the rounded rules.

No claim of universal superiority, automatic model detection, formal
proof-assistant verification, or a proved asymptotic product law is
made.

\section{Limitations}

The main structural family is centered and one-dimensional. The shifted
test covers only small location mismatch and is not interval-certified.
The empirical separable error law is supplementary rather than
theoretical. A third-party implementation and specialist novelty review
remain desirable.

\section{Conclusion}

Partial moment constraints provide a controlled bridge between a
specialized positive Stieltjes minimax rule and polynomial-exact
quadrature. The hierarchy quantifies the price of protecting an unknown
smooth background. Weighted Chebyshev coefficient boxes convert the
trade-off into an exact robust risk and a model-aware selector. Interval
computation certifies the rounded rules over nearly the entire tested
domain.
